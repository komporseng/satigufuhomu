Table of Biography

Jennifer Lopez is an American actress, singer, dancer, fashion designer, producer, and businesswoman, also popular by her nickname J.Lo. Jennifer Lopez began appearing as a “Fly Girl” dancer in 1991 on “In Living Color”. There, she remained a regular until she decided to pursue an acting career in 1993.

Early Life and Childhood

Jennifer Lopez’s full name is Jennifer Lynn Lopez and she was born on July 24, 1969. She is currently 53 years old and her zodiac is Leo. She is originally from The Bronx borough of New York City and her parents are Puerto Rican.

Her mother is Guadalupe Rodríguez and her father is David López. Lopez has an older sister named Leslie, and a younger sister named Lynda, who is a journalist.

Her father worked the night shift at the Guardian Insurance Company before becoming a computer technician at the firm, while her mother was a homemaker. When she was born, the family was living in a small apartment in the Castle Hill neighborhood.

After a few years, her parents had saved up enough money to purchase a two-story house, which was considered a big deal for the relatively poor family.

At the age of five, she began taking singing and dancing lessons. She toured New York with her school when she was only seven years old. Additionally, her parents stressed the importance of work ethic and being able to speak English.

They encouraged all three daughters to put on performances at home, singing and dancing in front of each other and their friends so that they would stay “out of trouble”.

She spent her entire academic career in Catholic schools, finishing at Preston High School. While at school, she did gymnastics and ran track on a national level. She was also a member of the school’s softball team.

Rather than academically, J.Lo. excelled athletically, competing in national track championships. After high school, she attended Baruch College for just one semester.

Moreover, her nicknames include J. Lo, La Lopez, Lola, Jenny from the Block, La Guitarra, and La diva del Bronx. She is an American whereas her ethnic background is Puerto Rican.

Past Relationships

Jennifer Lopez and Alex Rodriguez both had a series of high-profile relationships before they found love with each other. As for her, she has been married three times. She wed producer Ojani Noa in February 1997, but the pair called it quits after only 11 months of marriage.

Following a two-year relationship with Sean “Diddy” Combs, Lopez and dancer Cris Judd tied the knot. The former spouses pulled the plug on their marriage after less than a year in June 2002 and they finalized their divorce in 2003.

Following her engagement to Ben Affleck, Lopez married Marc Anthony. The then-couple exchanged vows in June 2004. The couple welcomed a son and a daughter, Max, and Emme, on Long Island, on February 22, 2008.

The twins were introduced in the March 11, 2008, issue of People, for which the magazine paid a reported $6 million; the photographs of the twins became the most expensive celebrity picture ever taken at the time.

Three years after they welcomed their children, in July 2011, the couple announced their split, with Anthony filing for divorce in April 2012. They finalized their divorce on June 16, 2014, with Lopez retaining primary physical custody of the two children.

On 31 December 2014, J.Lo legally changed her name back to her, dropping Anthony’s last name (Muñiz). Months after her third divorce, Lopez started seeing backup dancer Beau “Casper” Smart. They ended their on-off relationship in 2016.

Professional Life

After a brief period of work as a dancer and actor in some stage musicals, she won a national competition in 1990 and earned a role as one of the Fly Girls in In Living Color, which is a Fox comedy series.

She appeared in some other television series, before debuting in Hollywood with the film My Family in 1995. The film enjoyed reasonable commercial and critical success.

Her breakthrough film came in 1997, in which she played the title role of Selena, a biopic of the singer Selena Quintanilla-Pérez, who died very young.

The same year Jennifer played one of the lead roles in the popular movies Anaconda and Blood and Wine. She also starred in the crime film Out of Sight in 1998, opposite Hollywood star George Clooney.

In the year 1999, she made her debut in music with the album On the 6, from which If You Had My Love became a huge success. Her second big hit came in 2000 with the single Waiting for Tonight and the same year, she starred in two films The Cell and Enough.

After 2000

In the year 2001, her singing career, as well as her success, reached new heights with her album J. Lo, the copies of which sold like hotcakes. The same year she starred in the commercially successful film The Wedding Planner.

She starred with Ben Affleck in the film Gigli and in 2004 she played the female lead in the film Shall We Dance? opposite Richard Gere.

In the year 2007, she starred with her then-husband Marc Anthony in the biographical film El Cantante. The film was a huge critical success. That year, she also released her Spanish album Como Ama Una Mujer.

The public received her Spanish album well and it also performed well on the pop charts. Now a celebrity, she was invited as one of the judges in the singing reality show American Idol in their eleventh season in January 2012.

However, in July, she departed from the show shortly after co-judge Steven Tyler left show. The same month, J.Lo released one of her extremely hit albums, ‘Dance Again… the Hits.

Her single If You Had My Love, from her debut album On the 6 reached no. 1 on the Billboard 100. They regarded the album as one of the most ‘infectious singles’ released that year.

Another song from the same album Waiting for Tonight became hugely popular and is considered her best single to date. The song reached number eight on the US Billboard Hot 100 and also earned her a Grammy nomination.

The film Selena in which she portrayed the title character, received amazing critical reviews, made with a budget of $20 million, and the film grossed $35 million at the box office.

Awards

In the year 1998, Jennifer Lopez earned a Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Musical or Comedy, for her role as the singer Selena in the movie of the same name.

Two of her singles, Waiting for Tonight and Let’s Get Loud, were nominated for Grammy Awards for Best Dance Recording in 2000.

Similarly, in the year 2003, her album, J to the L-O! The Remixes earned a place in the Guinness World Record for the first number-one remix album on the Billboard 200.

Once again in 2012, the Guinness World Record listed her name for her album On the Floor. The album was the Highest Viewed Female Music Video of All Time. She received her Star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in 2013.

Moreover, People magazine chose Lopez as one of the 50 Most Beautiful People in the World in the year 1997.
