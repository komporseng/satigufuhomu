August 2024 · 10 minute read

The stakes in Brad Anderson’s Blood (2022) are a touch too safe when they could have been a lot more provocative. As a result, the film loses all of its charm. The promises provided by the sudden dramatic change seem far too alluring to foresee the letdown that is to follow. However, the movie manages to drag you along for its spasmodic degeneration before bringing you to its wearying conclusion. Blood is a hauntingly desolate backdrop comprised of the rural landscape’s harsh indifference. In it, a mother on the verge of collapse is clinging to her last vestiges of hope.

The wonderful Michelle Monaghan expertly assumes the role of a mother struggling to deal with the mounting risks that pose a threat to her life and the lives of those she cares about. Unfortunately, the wonderful talent of the actress is lost on the mediocre horror. The tepid story gives the desperation that Monaghan faithfully evokes an unsteady foundation. If it had been a far more savage narrative about an unusual bloodthirst, the audience would have been filled with quaking fear at the impending peril. Furthermore, if the drama had continued, the realistic psychological horror clichés would have fuelled any remaining melancholy. Despite having a number of issues, the movie avoids receiving a “poor” rating. In actuality, the actors’ outstanding performances serve as the piece’s lone salvation, and they deserve all the credit for that.
