Chuck Seitsinger On Street Outlaws

Chuck Seitsinger is one of the OGs of Steet Outlaws. He got the inspiration to racing when his younger brother asked him to build a race car specifically for drag racing. Chuck managed to put together a 1989 Ford Mustang added with a Chevy engine under its hood.

He won the 2004 Nitrous Express P/4 XBOX Cup Championship in the 4 Cylinder division and was named Rookie of the Year by the NOPI Drag Racing Association. With his 89′ Ford Fox Body Mustang, Death Trap, Chuck has proven time and time again that he drives the quickest small tire car in the 405 with his 89′ Ford Fox Body Mustang.

“Racers were laughing when they found out I was from Oklahoma,” Chuck said. “We were asked questions like ‘do we still ride in wagons’ and ‘do we ride horses to work?’”The mocking didn’t last long after he placed second in the professional race.

Chuck said, “I answered their questions when I was number two qualifier at the first professional race I entered.”

But, Chuck started racing as soon as he was able to ride legally. He developed a passion for racing via racing motorcycles. Chuck started racing drag boats and then soon after racing cars.

He has also earned a name for himself as the race-master of the “No Prep Kings” series.

Chuck Seitsinger Net Worth

According to Street Outlaws Cast, Chuck Seitsinger has a total net worth of $650 thousand.

Besides his life as a street racer, he owns a successful repair shop. Plus, Chuck also heads the racing academy named Chuck Seitsinger Racing. His company website sells merch such as hoodies, caps, OG Shirt, Death Trap diecast, Beanie, and many more.

Before drag racing, he worked as a Honda mechanic. After working on a Honda Civic hatchback, Chuck entered a race in Virginia. He shared, “So, it just made sense that I give it a shot”.

He also has a YouTube channel that flaunts 31.5k subscribers at this moment.

Does Chuck Seitsinger Have A Wife?

Chuck Seitsinger is no longer a married man. But, he was previously married to his former wife Valerie Butcher Shiff. The pair had celebrated their 2013 Christmas together and Chuck posted on his Twitter, “Merry Christmas from my family to yours, Chuck, Valerie, Chelsea, Madelyn & Miranda”.

In 2016, Chuck had written on his Facebook, “Well I guess it’s back to court with my jealous ex, I need a good divorce attorney. Not a nice one, I want the meanest, best in Oklahoma!!! Send them my way!!!”.

But in July 2017, Chuck revealed on his Facebook post, “At what point is enough??? I have been divorced since 2014, my ex always running my name in the dirt so she can get pitty from her 5 friends on Facebook, my kids are at filming with me and she is always blasting me, you think she would get a life, my kids are getting older and their mom is embarrassing them”.

Together, Chuck and his now-former wife, Valerie shared three daughters; Chelsea, Miranda, and Madelyn (17 years old). Chelsea the eldest daughter attended Oklahoma State University where he studied veterinary medicine. She works at Embassy Suites by Hilton while living in Norman, Oklahoma.

Whereas, their two younger daughters are featured on his socials multiple times.

Chuck is a family man and is very close to his all three daughters. Fans of Chuck has also praised him for being a role model of a father. He recently posted a picture of himself with his three daughters in late January 2022 on his Facebook. He wrote there, “As a father of 3 daughters I love traveling all over the country racing 1 to 7 times a week, but when you been gone for 5 weeks doing what we do, I miss being home seeing my girls and hanging out”.

Following the divorce, Valerie is now a married woman. Her Facebook revealed that she shared her wedding vows with her husband on 2 September 2018. She is currently working as an Educator at Artistic Nail Design since April 2017. Hailing from Del City, Oklahoma, she currently lives in Oklahoma City.

Valerie is also available on Twitter (@vals10nails).
