Anna Delvey

June 2024 · 5 minute read

Anna Delvey, the fictitious German heiress, has had several identities over the course of her 30 years. She’s been a Russian immigrant, a fashion student, a New York socialite, an arts and culture entrepreneur, a Rikers Island inmate, a wealthy celebrity defendant, a convicted felon, and the subject of Netflix’s Inventing Anna.

What is the net worth of Anna Delvey?

Anna Sorokin’s net worth today is far from the $60 million she once pretended to have in offshore bank accounts as Anna Delvey. While Sorokin was paid by Netflix for the rights to her life story for their Inventing Anna limited series, the disgraced scammer doesn’t have nearly as much money left to her name after paying back her restitution and ongoing legal fees.

Assuming she paid a total of $223,000 in combined restitution and state fines as reported by Insider, Sorokin would be left with around $97,000 from her Inventing Anna paycheck. As such, Anna Delvey’s net worth in 2022 sits below $100,000—but given her past spending habits, we wouldn’t be surprised if she’s already in the red.

Childhood and education

Anna Delvey, whose real name is Anna Sorokin, was born on January 23, 1991, in Domodedovo, Russian SFSR, and is an Aquarius. The con artist, who is 31 years old, is German by nationality and Russian by ethnicity. In June 2011, she graduated from the Bischöfliche Liebfrauenschule Eschweiler, a Catholic elementary school in Eschweller, and went on to Central Saint Martins, an art school, where she dropped out and returned to Germany.

Family background

Anna Delvey grew up in a middle-class household. Vadim, her father, was a truck driver, and her mother was the owner of a small convenience store. Delvey’s family moved to North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany, when she was 16 years old.Anna Delvey’s father worked as an executive at a transport company in Germany before opening an HVAC company that specialized in energy efficiency. Her parents have now relocated to the German town of Duran.

Anna Delvey’s father, Vadim Sorokin, stated in interviews that he has disowned his daughter after she was found guilty of grand larceny and other charges in 2019.

When did Anna Delvey get out of prison?

No, Anna Delvey isn’t still behind bars. At the very least, she won’t be in the same jail as when she was first convicted in 2019. After serving three years of her four-to-12-year sentence, Delvey was granted parole in February 2021. What caused her to be released so soon? Excellent demeanor.

Delvey immediately paid off all of her debts after being released. She paid back the $200,000 she owed the banks with a share of the $320,000 she earned from Netflix for the rights to her life story. In addition, she had to pay $25,000 in state fines in New York.After her obligations were settled, Delvey checked into the NoMad Hotel in central Manhattan and proceeded to share beautiful photos of her post-prison life on Instagram. She gave a number of interviews and made some bold statements about her time behind bars. Her stay in Rikers Island was “therapeutic,” she remarked on Good Morning America.

Delvey relocated to Manhattan and began advertising a prospective new fashion collaboration with Paul Cupo, the former designer of Hood By Air. She maintained regular contact with her parole officer. Unfortunately, it was just temporary.

Reason for re arrested

In March 2021, immigration officers detained Anna Delvey for overstaying her visa. She had an immigration detainer against her name when she was first released from prison, but she wasn’t in any hurry to leave New York.Delvey was supposed to be deported back to Germany on March 26, but she was in jail stateside while filing an application for relief, which is legalese for “please don’t make me go back to Germany.”

In June 2021, however, her asylum application was denied, but she swiftly appealed. Despite her lawyer’s multiple attempts to get her released on bond as she awaits a final judgement on her appeal, she remained in jail at the Orange County Correctional Facility in upstate New York. She was isolated for more than a week after testing positive for COVID-19 in January.

Is Anna Delvey Going to Be Deported?

No way! Not yet! Delvey was supposed to fly to Frankfurt on March 14, 2022, but her deportation was thwarted at the last minute when she simply did not show up, according to the New York Post.Anna Delvey’s lawyer, Manny Arora, has now indicated that her failure to appear was most likely due to the deportation application he filed late Monday afternoon. Evidently, a court must make a formal judgement on the case before Delvey may be deported.

Consider this one of those few times when missing a flight is actually a good thing, because the bogus heiress has made it clear that she does not want to return to Germany.
