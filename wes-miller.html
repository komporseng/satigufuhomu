Wes Miller- Biography

Wes Miller is the University of North Carolina at Greensboro’s industrious and respected American basketball coach. He is presently the Southern Conference’s longest-serving head coach. He was named Southern Conference Coach of the Year twice, in 2012 and 2018.

Wes Miller- Professional Career

He began his career as a member of the James Madison Dukes Men’s Basketball team before being moved to the University of North Carolina. He played a season for the London Capitals of the British Basketball League after graduating. He played professionally for England for a year before being appointed as an assistant coach at Elon University. Later, he was hired by High Point University and joined the staff of Scott Cherry, the new head coach.

In 2010, he accepted a position as an assistant coach at the University of North Carolina in Greensboro. He went on to become the head coach at the College of Idaho. In 2011, he took over as temporary head coach for the Spartans. He put forth a lot of effort with the club and helped them win first place in the North Division of the Southern Conference. Miller was named as the full-time head coach of the University of North Carolina Greensboro on March 6, 2012. On the 29th of December 2018, he was named UNCG’s all-time leading scorer with 125 victories.
